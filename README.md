# Marketing Cohort & Retention Analytics | Customer Lifetime Value & Channel Performance 📊

## 📌 Executive Summary
This project analyzes **~740,000 transactions** from an e-commerce business (2009–2011) to understand customer retention, cohort behavior, and marketing channel effectiveness. By leveraging **Power BI** for advanced business intelligence and **AI-Assisted workflows (Prompt Engineering)** to accelerate the initial data extraction (Python/SQL), I built an end-to-end analytics pipeline that segments customers by acquisition cohort, calculates retention rates over time, and quantifies the financial value generated by each marketing channel.

Key findings: **The December 2009 cohort is the most valuable** with an average LTV of **$1,840**, while the **"Email" channel drives the highest revenue per customer ($1,245)** despite lower volume. The retention heatmap reveals a clear drop-off after month 3, highlighting a critical opportunity for early engagement campaigns.

## 🔍 Key Business Findings & Recommendations

### 1. Retention Drops Sharply After Month 3
Across all cohorts, the average retention rate falls from **100% (month 1)** to **~35% (month 3)** and stabilizes around **20% by month 6**.  
**Recommendation:** Implement a targeted post-purchase email sequence during the critical first 90 days to boost repeat purchases and mitigate early churn.

### 2. Email Channel Delivers the Highest-Value Customers
Customers acquired via **Email** spend **$1,245 on average**, **2.3x more** than those from **Social Media ($540)**.  
**Recommendation:** Reallocate budget towards email acquisition campaigns and test referral programs within this segment to replicate this high-value behavior.

### 3. The December 2009 Cohort is an Outlier in LTV
The cohort that started in **December 2009** has an average LTV of **$1,840**, nearly **50% higher** than the average of subsequent cohorts. This suggests a highly successful holiday promotion or a particularly loyal customer batch.  
**Recommendation:** Perform a deep-dive analysis on the marketing mix and product offerings used in Dec-2009 to replicate its success in future holiday strategies.
